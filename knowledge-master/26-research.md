# Research

Systematic investigation to discover, interpret, or revise knowledge — methodologies, frameworks, and best practices.

## Research Types

**Primary Research:** Original data collection. Surveys, experiments, interviews, observation, case studies.

**Secondary Research:** Analyze existing data. Literature reviews, meta-analyses, systematic reviews.

**Qualitative:** Why and how. Rich, contextual understanding. Interviews, ethnography, thematic analysis.

**Quantitative:** What and how much. Statistical significance. Experiments, surveys with numerical data, A/B testing.

**Mixed Methods:** Combine qualitative and quantitative. Triangulation strengthens findings.

## Process

1. **Define the question:** What exactly do you want to know? Clear question = clear answer.
2. **Literature review:** What's already known? Where are the gaps?
3. **Design:** Methodology, sample, instruments, variables, controls.
4. **Collect data:** Systematic, ethical, documented.
5. **Analyze:** Statistical or thematic. Look for patterns, outliers, contradictions.
6. **Interpret:** What does this mean? Limitations? Implications?
7. **Share:** Paper, report, presentation, actionable summary.

## Key Skills

- **Skepticism:** Question everything, including your own conclusions
- **Source evaluation:** Authority, accuracy, currency, bias, relevance
- **Pattern recognition:** See connections across disparate data
- **Intellectual honesty:** Follow evidence where it leads, even if uncomfortable
- **Precision:** Define terms clearly. Avoid vague language.

## Information Sources

- **Primary:** Original papers, raw data, patents, legal documents
- **Secondary:** Reviews, textbooks, news analysis, encyclopedias
- **Tertiary:** Indexes, databases, bibliographies, compilations

## Common Pitfalls

- Confirmation bias: selecting evidence that supports your hypothesis
- P-hacking: manipulating analysis to get significant results
- Small sample sizes: conclusions don't generalize
- Correlation ≠ causation: third variable problem
- Publication bias: positive results get published more
- Survivorship bias: looking at successes, ignoring failures

## Practical Research for Daily Life

- Google Scholar for academic papers
- PubMed for medical/health research
- ArXiv for AI/ML/CS papers
- SSRN for social sciences
- Wikipedia as a starting point (check citations)
- Books for depth, papers for cutting edge, blogs for practical application
