# Dark Psychology

The study of manipulation, persuasion, and control — how people exploit psychological principles for selfish or harmful ends.

## Key Concepts

**Manipulation:** Deliberate influence through deceptive or underhanded tactics. Gaslighting, guilt-tripping, love bombing, breadcrumbing.

**Gaslighting:** Making someone doubt their own perception, memory, or sanity. Pattern: deny, minimize, blame, twist.

**Narcissistic Abuse Cycle:** Idealize → Devalue → Discard → Hoover. Pattern repeats. Victim becomes addicted to the idealization phase.

**Dark Triad:** Subclinical narcissism (grandiose sense of self), Machiavellianism (strategic exploitation), psychopathy (lack of empathy, impulsivity).

## Common Tactics

- **Foot-in-the-door:** Small request → larger request
- **Door-in-the-face:** Large request (refused) → smaller (real target)
- **Scarcity:** "Limited time" creates artificial urgency
- **Social proof:** "Everyone is doing it"
- **Authority bias:** People defer to perceived experts
- **Reciprocity pressure:** Give something to trigger obligation

## Defense Mechanisms

- Set boundaries early — test them, enforce them
- Trust patterns, not words — consistency is the only signal
- Slow down — urgency is a manipulation flag
- External perspective — what would a neutral observer say?
- Document everything — memory is unreliable under pressure

## Ethics

Understanding dark psychology is defense, not offense. Knowing how manipulation works lets you spot it — in others and sometimes in yourself.
