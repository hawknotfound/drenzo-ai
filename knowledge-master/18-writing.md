# Writing

Clear, effective written communication — across formats, audiences, and purposes.

## Foundations

**Clarity over cleverness.** The goal is understanding, not admiration. Short words, short sentences, short paragraphs.

**Show, don't tell.** Instead of "It was scary", describe the cold sweat, the racing heart, the shadow moving.

**Kill your darlings.** Cut everything that doesn't serve the purpose. Every sentence must earn its place.

**Active voice.** "The dog bit the man" beats "The man was bitten by the dog."

## Formats

**Blog/Article:** Headline → hook → context → body → conclusion → CTA. Scannable with subheadings.

**Copywriting:** Persuasive, benefit-focused. Headline grabs, bullet points convince, CTA closes.

**Technical:** Precise, structured, example-driven. Assume reader has context but needs clarity.

**Creative:** Voice-driven, evocative, rhythmic. Rules exist to be bent only after they're mastered.

**Academic/Research:** Thesis → evidence → analysis → conclusion. Citations, objectivity, structure.

## Process

1. Think: What's the one thing you want them to remember?
2. Outline: Structure before words. Skeleton before flesh.
3. Write: First draft is allowed to be terrible. Get it out.
4. Edit: Remove, restructure, rewrite. Editing is where writing happens.
5. Review: Read aloud. Wait a day. Get feedback.

## Style

- Tone should match audience and context
- Consistency in terminology, formatting, voice
- Rhythm: Vary sentence length for impact
- End strong — last sentence of each section should land
