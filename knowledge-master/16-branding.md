# Branding

The perception people have of a product, person, or organization. Not just visuals — every interaction builds the brand.

## Brand Identity

**Visual Identity:** Logo, color palette, typography, imagery style, design system. Must be distinctive, scalable, memorable.

**Verbal Identity:** Tone of voice, messaging hierarchy, key phrases, naming conventions. How the brand speaks.

**Brand Personality:** Human traits associated with the brand. Arrogant? Helpful? Rebel? Authoritative? Must be intentional.

**Brand Promise:** What the customer can consistently expect. Delivered through every touchpoint.

## Brand Strategy

- **Positioning:** Where you sit in the market relative to competitors
- **Differentiation:** What makes you genuinely different (not just better)
- **Audience:** Who you serve, who you don't. Niche density beats broad dilution.
- **Story:** The narrative arc that makes your brand meaningful and memorable

## Brand Architecture

- **Monolithic:** One brand for everything (Google, Apple)
- **Endorsed:** Sub-brands with parent backing (Microsoft Office, Marriott Hotels)
- **House of Brands:** Independent brands under one parent (Unilever, P&G)

## Brand Equity

Awareness → Associations → Perceived Quality → Loyalty → Advocacy. Strong equity = pricing power, customer retention, competitive protection.

## Identity Design Principles

- Simple survives. Over-designed dates fast.
- Consistency builds recognition. Change is expensive.
- Emotional connection > feature explanation.
- A brand is what people say when you leave the room.
