# Learning

How to learn effectively — acquire, retain, and apply knowledge and skills.

## Learning Models

**Feynman Technique:** Teach a concept in plain language to identify gaps in your own understanding.

**Bloom's Taxonomy:** Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create. Higher levels require deeper engagement.

**Dreyfus Model:** Novice → Advanced Beginner → Competent → Proficient → Expert. Skill acquisition stages. Each stage needs different instruction.

**Kolb's Cycle:** Concrete Experience → Reflective Observation → Abstract Conceptualization → Active Experimentation.

## Effective Techniques (High Utility)

- **Active recall:** Test yourself instead of re-reading. Most effective single technique.
- **Spaced repetition:** Review at increasing intervals. Anki, SuperMemo, FSRS.
- **Interleaving:** Mix different topics within a session. Improves discrimination.
- **Elaboration:** Explain why and how things connect. Deeper encoding.
- **Dual coding:** Combine verbal and visual representations.

## Learning Pipeline

1. **Input:** Read, watch, listen, observe. Active engagement, not passive consumption.
2. **Process:** Write notes in your own words. Connect to existing knowledge. Question assumptions.
3. **Apply:** Use it. Build something. Teach someone. Solve a real problem.
4. **Reflect:** What worked? What didn't? What's the next layer?
5. **Repeat:** Learning is a loop, not a destination.

## Note-Taking Systems

- **Zettelkasten:** Atomic notes linked by context. Ideas compound.
- **Progressive Summarization:** Layer summaries on top of raw notes.
- **Building a Second Brain (CODE):** Capture, Organize, Distill, Express.

## Meta-Learning

Learning how to learn is its own skill. Invest in it early. The map is not the territory — adapt methods to your brain.
