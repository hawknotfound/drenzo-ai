# Communication

Exchanging information effectively — speaking, writing, listening, and reading the room.

## Core Skills

**Active Listening:** Listen to understand, not to reply. Paraphrase back. Ask clarifying questions. Pause before responding.

**Clarity:** Know what you want to say before you say it. Simple language. One point at a time.

**Empathy:** Understand their perspective before advocating yours. Validate feelings even when you disagree.

**Assertiveness:** State your needs directly without aggression. "I feel X when Y because Z. I need W."

## Communication Styles

- **Passive:** Avoid conflict, suppress needs, build resentment
- **Aggressive:** Win at others' expense, damage relationships
- **Passive-Aggressive:** Indirect resistance, sarcasm, subtle digs
- **Assertive (sweet spot):** Direct, honest, respectful of both parties

## Difficult Conversations

1. Start with intent: "I want to talk about X because I value our relationship"
2. State facts without judgment: "When X happened..."
3. Share your perspective: "I felt/thought..."
4. Ask for theirs: "What was your experience?"
5. Problem-solve together: "How do we move forward?"

## Non-Verbal Communication

- Tone: 38% of message. Posture, eye contact, gestures, proximity
- Mirroring: Builds rapport naturally
- Microexpressions: Brief, involuntary facial expressions
- Silence: Powerful tool. Creates space for thought, signals confidence

## Written Communication

- Subject line = thesis statement
- First paragraph = executive summary
- Structure for scanning — headings, bullets, bold key points
- Tone: Formal for authority, warm for connection, direct for action
- Proofread once for typos, once for clarity, once for brevity
