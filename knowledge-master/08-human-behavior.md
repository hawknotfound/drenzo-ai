# Human Behavior

Why people do what they do — the observable patterns, motivations, and triggers behind actions.

## Motivation Drivers

**Intrinsic vs Extrinsic:** Internal satisfaction vs external rewards. Overjustification effect — rewards can kill intrinsic motivation.

**Maslow's Hierarchy:** Physiological → Safety → Belonging → Esteem → Self-actualization. Later revised to include transcendence.

**Self-Determination Theory:** Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness — three universal psychological needs.

## Behavioral Patterns

- **Habit formation:** Cue → Craving → Response → Reward (Loop). Atomic Habits framework.
- **Procrastination:** Present self vs future self conflict. Emotion regulation problem, not time management.
- **Status seeking:** Social ranking is a primary driver. Conspicuous consumption, signaling theory.
- **Reciprocity:** We feel obliged to return favors. Door-in-the-face, foot-in-the-door techniques.

## Group Behavior

- Social loafing: individuals exert less in groups
- Groupthink: consensus over critical evaluation
- Deindividuation: loss of self-awareness in crowds
- Bystander effect: diffusion of responsibility

## Reading People

- Watch actions, not words — consistency check
- Emotional cues: microexpressions, tone shifts, word choice
- Defensiveness patterns: deflection, minimization, counterattack
