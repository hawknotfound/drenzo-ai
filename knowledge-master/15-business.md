# Business

How value is created, delivered, and captured — strategy, operations, models, and growth.

## Business Models

**SaaS:** Subscription software. Predictable recurring revenue. High margins, high CAC.

**Marketplace:** Connect buyers and sellers. Network effects. Chicken-and-egg problem early.

**Platform:** Users build value on top. Ecosystem dynamics. Winner-take-most tendencies.

**Direct-to-Consumer (D2C):** Own the customer relationship. No middlemen. Brand control.

**Freemium:** Free tier acquires users, premium tier monetizes. Conversion rate matters.

## Strategy

- **Porter's Five Forces:** Industry rivalry, supplier power, buyer power, threat of substitutes, new entrants
- **Blue Ocean:** Create new market space instead of competing in existing one
- **Flywheel:** Accumulate momentum — each success feeds the next
- **Moats:** Network effects, brand, switching costs, scale advantages, IP

## Metrics

- **Unit economics:** CAC (customer acquisition cost), LTV (lifetime value), payback period
- **Growth:** MRR/ARR, churn rate, net revenue retention, activation rate
- **Profitability:** Gross margin, operating margin, contribution margin
- **Health:** Burn multiple, runway, magic number (efficiency)

## Pricing

Cost-plus, value-based, competitor-based. SaaS tiers should map to customer segments. Price is a signal of quality.

## Scaling

Product-market fit first. Then: hire, systematize, delegate. Culture decays as you grow — preserve it intentionally.
