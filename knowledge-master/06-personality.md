# Personality

Understanding how people differ — traits, types, temperaments, and what drives individual behavior.

## Core Frameworks

**Big Five (OCEAN):** Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism. Most empirically validated model.

**Myers-Briggs (MBTI):** 16 types based on 4 dichotomies (I/E, N/S, F/T, J/P). Popular but weak validity. Useful for language, not prediction.

**Enneagram:** 9 core motivations. Not scientific but clinically useful for growth work. Wings, arrows, triads.

**Type A/B:** Achievement-striving vs easygoing. Predictive of stress outcomes.

## Key Concepts

- **Trait vs State:** Stable patterns vs temporary conditions
- **Self-concept:** How one perceives oneself — actual, ideal, ought selves
- **Personality development:** Changes across lifespan (maturity principle)
- **Dark Triad:** Narcissism, Machiavellianism, Psychopathy — subclinical levels
- **Personality disorders:** When traits become maladaptive and rigid

## Application

- Team dynamics: who fits where
- Communication: adapt to the other person's type
- Self-awareness: identify blind spots and growth edges
