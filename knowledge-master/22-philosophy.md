# Philosophy

Fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, mind, and meaning.

## Branches

**Metaphysics:** What is real? Reality, existence, objects, time, causality, free will.

**Epistemology:** What is knowledge? Belief, truth, justification, skepticism, sources of knowledge.

**Ethics:** What is right and wrong? Normative ethics (deontology, consequentialism, virtue ethics), meta-ethics, applied ethics.

**Logic:** What is valid reasoning? Deductive, inductive, abductive. Formal and informal fallacies.

**Aesthetics:** What is beauty? Art, taste, judgment, the sublime.

**Political Philosophy:** What is a just society? Liberty, equality, authority, rights, justice.

## Key Thinkers & Schools

**Ancient:** Socrates (questioning), Plato (forms, ideal state), Aristotle (logic, virtue ethics, causation).

**Stoicism:** Marcus Aurelius, Seneca, Epictetus. Control what you can, accept what you can't. Memento mori, amor fati.

**Existentialism:** Kierkegaard, Nietzsche, Sartre, Camus. Existence precedes essence. Meaning is created, not given.

**Eastern:** Buddhism (suffering and liberation), Taoism (wu wei, harmony), Confucianism (virtue, social harmony), Advaita (non-duality).

**Modern:** Kant (categorical imperative), Mill (utilitarianism), Hegel (dialectic), Wittgenstein (language games), Foucault (power/knowledge).

## Practical Philosophy

- First principles reasoning: break down to fundamental truths, rebuild
- Stoicism: focus on what's within your control
- Existentialism: you are responsible for the meaning you create
- Pragmatism: truth is what works in practice
- Philosophical skepticism: question assumptions systematically
